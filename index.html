Courses We Offer

Howard University offers diverse programs across arts, sciences, business, engineering, law, medicine, and social work, with a strong focus on social justice, African American studies, and global leadership.

Intermediate

At Howard University, intermediate courses serve as a crucial bridge between introductory and advanced studies, offering students the opportunity to deepen their understanding of specific subjects. These courses are designed to build on foundational knowledge, providing more complex and specialized content across various disciplines, including the arts, sciences, business, engineering, and social sciences. Whether pursuing degrees in law, medicine, or liberal arts, intermediate courses at Howard are essential for students aiming to refine their skills, engage in critical thinking, and prepare for advanced coursework or professional practice. These courses often include rigorous assessments and practical applications, ensuring that students are well-prepared for the challenges of higher-level studies and their future careers.

Degree

A degree course at Howard University is a comprehensive program of study that leads to an academic degree, such as a bachelor's, master's, or doctorate. These courses are structured to provide in-depth knowledge and specialized skills in a chosen field, whether it's in the arts, sciences, business, law, engineering, medicine, or social sciences. Degree courses at Howard University combine theoretical learning with practical experiences, including research projects, internships, and hands-on training, depending on the discipline. Students engage in a series of core and elective courses that progressively build their expertise, culminating in the completion of a degree that prepares them for professional careers or further academic pursuits. Howard’s degree offerings span a broad range of disciplines, including business, law, medicine, engineering, the arts, social sciences, and the humanities

Post Graduation

Post-graduation at Howard University refers to advanced academic programs that students pursue after completing an undergraduate degree. These programs include master's, doctoral, and professional degrees across various disciplines such as law, medicine, business, engineering, education, and the arts. Howard's post-graduate programs are designed to provide specialized knowledge, research opportunities, and professional skills, preparing students for leadership roles in their fields. The curriculum often involves a combination of advanced coursework, independent research, internships, and thesis or dissertation projects, allowing students to deepen their expertise and contribute to their chosen professions or academic communities. Post-graduate programs at Howard emphasize the application of knowledge to address global and community challenges

Our Facilities

Howard University offers a variety of state-of-the-art facilities to enhance the academic and social experiences of its students. These include advanced classrooms, well-equipped research centers,
and extensive library resources, such as the renowned Founders Library. The campus provides a range of housing options and dining facilities that cater to diverse needs.

World Class Library

Howard University’s library system, highlighted by the historic Founders Library, offers diverse collections and digital resources, focusing on African American history and culture. With modern technology, varied study spaces, and extensive online access, the libraries support individual and collaborative learning. Librarians provide essential services like research assistance, fostering a rich academic environment.

Largest Play Ground

Howard University’s playgrounds and athletic facilities include fields and courts for sports like football, soccer, basketball, and tennis. They support both intramural and varsity activities, offering students diverse opportunities for fitness and recreation.

The Biggest Cafeteria

Howard University’s cafeteria offers a variety of dining options that cater to diverse dietary needs and preferences. Students can enjoy a range of cuisines and healthy choices in a vibrant and social environment. The cafeteria provides meal plans, grab-and-go options, and special dietary accommodations, ensuring all students have access to nutritious meals.
